Methods & references

Every specimen page in this lab obeys the same measurement rules and cites the same handful of sources. They live here once, so the pages stay slim and the definitions can't drift apart. The per-model pages hold the numbers and the arguments; this holds the vocabulary they're written in.

§1How a sweep is measured

A sweep duplicates or prunes transformer blocks — no training — and asks whether capability moved. The whole difficulty is telling a real shift from seed noise, a generous grader, or the sheer number of configs tried. These are the rules that keep an answer honest.

Paired Cohen's d vs paired t
Every config is scored against an in-run baseline on the same seeds, so the test is the per-seed difference, not the marginal spread. d = mean(diffs) / std(diffs) is the seed-count-independent effect size; t ≈ d·√n grows with seed count. Real-but-underpowered candidates show high d, low t — worth more seeds, not yet proven.
The r-gauge
std(per-seed Δ) / std(baseline seed-noise). r < 1 → pairing cancels common-mode seed noise, so trust d even when the config's raw mean sits inside the baseline's spread (overlapping ranges are not "it's noise" — the paired diff is the test). r ≈ 1 → the difference noise is seed-independent; the config is riding the discovery noise floor and more seeds won't rescue it.
The multiplicity budget
A sweep of ~150–500 config×channel tests expects ~25 false positives at p<0.05 by chance alone. A lone t≈1.7–2.3 "discovery" is at that look-elsewhere floor, not above it. A result has to clear the budget — or be corroborated on another axis — before it counts.
Strict over partial
Strict grading (exact-equality, position-agnostic any-match on the answer integer) is primary. Partial credit — David Ng's pad-and-relative- difference scorer — rewards an answer for being close in magnitude, and it has manufactured phantom leaderboard champions on every model in this lab. It captures a real second axis (closeness) but is never folded into the headline. Writeups lead with strict + strict-correct %.
The grader-gaming taxonomy
Every model's first leaderboard is partly phantom, always the same way — a lenient grader rewards output form over correctness. Three faces: partial-credit inflation (close-but-wrong tidy numbers score ~0.99), echo contamination (the parser grabs the prompt's own numbers when the model just echoes), and compliance-not-capability (pruning unclogs a stalled output valve so the model finally emits a clean token — a real gain only if the latent knowledge was there). The fix is an operand-aware any-match strict regrade on the preserved verbose responses.
Seeds vs probes
Orthogonal. Seeds = precision (replication of the same battery); probes = coverage (breadth of what's tested). Bulking seeds on a thin battery makes it precisely narrow, not representative. Seed budget goes to the seed-independent channel — the one where pairing can't already cancel the noise.
Sampler & quant discipline
One fixed low-variance sampler per sweep (temp 0.3 + engine defaults, seed-controlled — greedy n=1 fabricates false-positives). A uniform sampler or quant level is a constant offset that cancels in seed-paired diffs; the only thing that can distort a ranking (versus an effect-size magnitude) is a sampler×config or quant×surgery interaction. So low-bit quant noise is real — it degrades the precision tails (exact arithmetic, tool-call tokens, long context) — but it is not a blocker for the broad-strokes capability a sweep measures. Magnitude of change is not sensitivity to change — the same distinction that separates a layer's geometric activity from its behavioural fragility.

Per-model specifics (quantisation, layer count, the per-layer-array reindexer, exact seed and probe counts) live in each specimen's own method note. This is the shared vocabulary, not the run log.

§3Colophon & credits

Credit & method

The duplication technique is David Ng's RYS ("Repeat Your Self"). The no-chain-of-thought, tiny-output, partial-credit probe design is also his, and it elegantly sidesteps three confounds at once — the indecision tax, format brittleness, and sample-size starvation. The strict-equality regrade used throughout this lab doesn't refute that design; it extends it. Every architecture has its own neuroanatomy, and that framing is his too.

Models are the property of their respective authors under their respective licenses. Probing, scoring, plots, and prose are the author's — built and run locally on a 16 GB Mac mini, and written up as it happened. Where a model did especially heavy lifting on a specimen — greenfield builds, deep troubleshooting — that page names the humans and models who did its work.
